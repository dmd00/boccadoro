L'azienda

Three generations.
One pursuit.

I
The Land

Puglia is already world renowned for its bountiful harvest — and even amongst this already excellent crop, our oil is among the best in the world.

II
The Harvest

Each October, we begin harvesting — picking the olives by hand, taking only the best, and collecting them for pressing. No pesticides, no chemicals. Just olives, and nothing else.

III
The Oil

Each bottle is made from one grove, in one place — no mixing across countries, no changing flavor profiles. Ultra-premium quality, and you can taste the difference.

A note on availability

"Some harvests are more bountiful than others, and some are less. We only sell what we produce — so when we run out, we run out."

Every bottle of Bocca d'Oro is the product of a single season, a single grove, and the hands of our family. There is no restocking from another source. If you find it, don't wait.

La nostra storia

A family rooted
in the grove.

Mario and his wife came to America from Serracapriola, a small town in Puglia, Italy, with their little son in tow — seeking gainful employment and following the American dream, but never leaving their Italian roots behind. The farm stayed within the family, and only those close to them worked the land while he was away.

Frequent trips back home to tend to the orchard, participate in the harvest and processing, and bring back oil to their expanding family and newfound friends in America color the history of this oil. Mario always had pride in his oil and wanted to do more with it — but his focus was on his family and his work here in America first. He made a wonderful life for his children, his grandchildren, and his great-grandchildren.

Now, to honor his memory after his passing, the DiCesares are sharing the oil with the world — so that others may come to enjoy it with their own family meals.

"Three generations of DiCesares have collaborated to bring this oil to the US, and for good reason."

— The DiCesare Family
